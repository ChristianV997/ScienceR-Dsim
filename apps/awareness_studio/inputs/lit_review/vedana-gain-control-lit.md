*Based on articles retrieved from PubMed. DOI links provided for all citations.*

## Key Hypothesis Link

The **vedana gain-control model** proposes that feeling-tone (vedanā — pleasant / unpleasant / neutral valence) acts as a modulatory gain on attentional and emotional reactivity. In the simulation framework this maps to: vortex topology Qz ↔ gain-control variable; intensity I_mean ↔ signal amplitude after modulation.

## Article 1 — Williams et al. (2022): Mindfulness of Feeling Tone (Vedanā)

**Citation (PubMed PMID 35818377):**
Williams JMG, Baer R, Batchelor M, et al. "What Next After MBSR/MBCT? An Open Trial of an 8-Week Follow-on Program Exploring Mindfulness of Feeling Tone (Vedanā)."
*Mindfulness* 13(8):1931–1944. [DOI: 10.1007/s12671-022-01929-0](https://doi.org/10.1007/s12671-022-01929-0)

**Key findings:**
- 83 participants (78 with prior MBSR/MBCT) completed an 8-week vedanā-focused follow-on course
- Significant reductions: depression (d=0.56), stress (d=0.36), anxiety (d=0.53)
- Significant increases: well-being (d=0.54), mindfulness (d=0.65)
- 38% of participants met criteria for reliable change on anxiety/depression
- ~75% reported some unpleasant experiences; none reported harm

**Relevance to model:**
- Feeling-tone awareness training produces measurable gain-control effects on affective reactivity
- Effect sizes are mid-range (d=0.36–0.65), consistent with partial modulation rather than suppression
- Oxford group (Williams & colleagues) — this is the primary clinical validation of vedanā as a trainable skill

**Keywords:** Feeling tone, Mindfulness-based program, Vedanā, MBSR, MBCT

## Article 2 — Messina et al. (2021): Acceptance and PCC Deactivation

**Citation (PubMed PMID 33475715):**
Messina I, Grecucci A, Viviani R. "Neurobiological models of emotion regulation: a meta-analysis of neuroimaging studies of acceptance as an emotion regulation strategy."
*Soc Cogn Affect Neurosci* 16(3):257–267. [DOI: 10.1093/scan/nsab007](https://doi.org/10.1093/scan/nsab007)

**Key findings:**
- Meta-analysis of 13 fMRI studies (422 subjects, 170 foci) on acceptance vs. control
- **Posterior cingulate cortex (PCC)/precuneus deactivation** is the consistent neural substrate of acceptance, regardless of control condition
- High-level executive (prefrontal) processes are NOT a distinctive feature of acceptance
- Challenges top-down control models — acceptance works through PCC deactivation, not prefrontal up-regulation

**Relevance to model:**
- PCC/precuneus is a central DMN hub associated with self-referential processing and mind-wandering
- Acceptance (a core vedanā skill: observing feeling-tone without reactivity) deactivates the DMN default-mode
- This maps to the simulation: reduced vortex intensity (lower I_mean reactivity) without active suppression (low Qabs)
- Supports "gain-control via topology change" — Qz shift mediates PCC disengagement

**Keywords:** Acceptance, PCC, posterior cingulate, emotion regulation, DMN, meta-analysis

## Article 3 — Hölzel et al. (2011): Mechanisms of Mindfulness Meditation

**Citation (PubMed PMID 26168376):**
Hölzel BK, Lazar SW, Gard T, et al. "How Does Mindfulness Meditation Work? Proposing Mechanisms of Action From a Conceptual and Neural Perspective."
*Perspect Psychol Sci* 6(6):537–59. [DOI: 10.1177/1745691611419671](https://doi.org/10.1177/1745691611419671)

**Key findings:**
Four primary mechanisms with distinct neural substrates:
1. **Attention regulation** — ACC, prefrontal cortex
2. **Body awareness** — insula, temporo-parietal junction
3. **Emotion regulation** — via reappraisal, exposure, extinction, reconsolidation (fronto-limbic network)
4. **Change in self-perspective** — DMN, temporo-parietal junction

Neuroplastic changes documented in: ACC, insula, TPJ, fronto-limbic network, DMN.

**Relevance to model:**
- Body awareness (insula) is the primary portal for vedanā detection — the "gain sensor"
- Emotion regulation via exposure/extinction = gain-control in action (not suppression, but modulation)
- Self-perspective change = reduced default-mode reactivity → lower Qabs in the topology model
- Canonical mechanistic framework; situates the simulation model within established neuroscience

**Keywords:** Attention regulation, body awareness, emotion regulation, neuroplasticity, insula, DMN, ACC
