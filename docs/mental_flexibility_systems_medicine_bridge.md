# Mental Flexibility & Systems Medicine Bridge

## Purpose
P34 adds a claim-governed research bridge from the ToL / ScienceR-Dsim theory stack into mental flexibility, body-brain-mind observables, ML feature schemas, safety tiers, clinical translation boundaries, validation protocols, and command-center status artifacts.

## Why P34 follows ToL / RAG / command-center work
The prior ToL digestion, RAG gateway, command-center API contract, local ops, DS005620 readiness, and ontology guardrails created enough artifact structure to map theory into constrained research artifacts without crossing into public medical claims.

## Mental flexibility model
Mental flexibility is modeled as the measurable capacity to lower exit-cost rigidity, widen perceived affordances, recover after perturbation, maintain embodied regulation, and reduce compulsive self-binding.

## Machine-learning role
ML is limited to consented feature organization, hypothesis testing, reporting, drift monitoring, and future validation workflows. It must not infer labels, fabricate targets, infer protected traits, or operate clinically without locked-model evaluation and human oversight.

## Body-brain-mind medicine
The bridge separates body, brain, mind, behavior, and environment layers. Each layer is a partial observable; no single telemetry stream is sufficient for diagnosis or clinical action.

## Disorder-to-dynamics matrix
The matrix uses candidate research mappings for depression, anxiety, trauma/PTSD, addiction, OCD, rumination, burnout, dissociation, and psychosis-risk domains. These are dynamics hypotheses only.

## Safety ladder
The ladder runs from research journaling through wellness, coaching-support, clinician-support candidate, digital therapeutic candidate, and medical-device candidate modes. Emergency/crisis markers require human/professional/emergency support and halt deep intervention.

## Clinical translation ladder
Research, wellness, coaching, clinician-support candidate, digital therapeutic candidate, and SaMD candidate modes remain explicitly separated with validation, risk control, privacy, bias, and regulatory gates.

## Validation protocols
Validation covers construct, predictive, safety, longitudinal, bias/fairness, privacy, escalation, human factors, drift, locked/adaptive model strategy, external validation, failure modes, and falsifiers.

## Command-center integration
`mental-health-bridge-command-center-payloads` writes `outputs/command_center/mock_payloads/mental_health_bridge_status.json` for Replit/frontend mock status use.

## Obsidian integration
`mental-health-bridge-sync-obsidian` mirrors the core P34 artifacts into `obsidian/07_ToL/` notes.

## Guardrails
The bridge blocks diagnosis claims, clinical efficacy claims, cure claims, clinician replacement, AI-therapist framing, emergency-care bypass, unsafe contemplative claims, and Q/Qabs/fdress proof claims.

## What this does not claim
It does not diagnose, claim clinical efficacy, claim cures, replace clinicians, provide medication advice, delete trauma, detect enlightenment, prove consciousness, prove suffering, prove soul, prove liberation, or promote ontology.

## Next research steps
Recommended next steps are a static artifact dashboard, expert review, protocol pre-registration, consent templates, privacy threat modeling, and small non-clinical validation pilots.
